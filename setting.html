Setting

About the Setting

Game of Thrones, based on the book series "A Song of Ice and Fire" by George R.R. Martin, is set in a medieval-inspired world primarily on the continents of Westeros and Essos.
The story is set in a time period reminiscent of the Middle Ages, featuring a blend of fantasy elements such as dragons, magic, and supernatural beings, alongside realistic political intrigue, warfare, and societal structures.
Westeros, where much of the action takes place, is divided into several regions, each ruled by noble houses vying for power and control.
The series begins at a time of relative stability, following the long summer season, but tensions rise rapidly as various factions vie for control of the Iron Throne, the seat of power in the Seven Kingdoms.

Beyond the Wall

Beyond the Wall is a vast, icy region located in the far north of Westeros. It is a harsh and unforgiving land, inhabited by various tribes known as the Free Folk or Wildlings. The Wall, a massive structure of ice and stone, separates the Seven Kingdoms from Beyond the Wall.

Life Beyond the Wall is characterized by constant struggle for survival, as the inhabitants face extreme cold, limited resources, and the constant threat of White Walkers and other supernatural creatures. The Night's Watch, an ancient order sworn to defend the realms of men, guards the Wall and protects the Seven Kingdoms from the dangers that lie beyond.

The North

The North is a vast and rugged region located in the northern part of Westeros. It is known for its harsh winters, dense forests, and towering mountains. The North is ruled by House Stark, one of the oldest and most respected noble houses in Westeros.

The people of the North, known as the Northmen or the Northerners, are hardy and resilient. They are fiercely loyal to House Stark and have a strong sense of honor and duty. The North is also home to the Night's Watch, an ancient order that guards the Wall and protects the realm from the dangers beyond.

The Iron Islands

The Iron Islands are a group of islands located off the western coast of Westeros. They are known for their rocky and unforgiving terrain, as well as their maritime culture and seafaring traditions.

The Iron Islands are ruled by House Greyjoy, a powerful and ancient noble house. The Ironborn, the inhabitants of the islands, are fierce and proud warriors who rely heavily on their naval strength.

The Iron Islands have a unique political and social structure, with a tradition of kingsmoots to elect their ruler. They are also known for their reaving and raiding activities, often targeting the coasts of Westeros and other nearby regions.

RiverLands

The Riverlands is a fertile region located in the central part of Westeros. It is crisscrossed by numerous rivers, including the Trident, which gives the region its name.

The Riverlands are known for their strategic importance, as they serve as a crossroads between the northern and southern parts of Westeros. The region has seen its fair share of conflict and has been the site of many battles throughout history.

House Tully, the ruling house of the Riverlands, holds the title of Lord Paramount of the Trident. The region is also home to other noble houses, such as House Frey and House Blackwood.

Vale of Arryn

The Vale of Arryn is a mountainous region located in the eastern part of Westeros. It is known for its breathtaking landscapes, including the towering mountains and the lush green valleys.

The Vale is ruled by House Arryn, one of the oldest and most prestigious noble houses in Westeros. The region is known for its strong adherence to the ancient traditions and customs of knighthood.

The Vale is also home to the Eyrie, a fortress perched atop a mountain peak, which serves as the seat of House Arryn. The Eyrie is considered one of the most impregnable castles in Westeros.

Westerlands

The Westerlands is a wealthy and resource-rich region located in the western part of Westeros. It is known for its abundant gold and silver mines, which have made House Lannister, the ruling house of the region, one of the wealthiest in the Seven Kingdoms.

The Westerlands are characterized by their rugged terrain, with rolling hills and rocky mountains. The region is also home to Casterly Rock, the ancestral seat of House Lannister.

House Lannister is known for its cunning and ambition, and they have played a significant role in the politics and power struggles of Westeros. The Westerlands are also home to other noble houses, such as House Crakehall and House Marbrand.

Crownlands

The Crownlands are a region located in the eastern part of Westeros, surrounding the capital city of King's Landing. The region is directly ruled by the Iron Throne and serves as the seat of power for the ruling monarch.

The Crownlands are known for their diverse population, as people from all over Westeros flock to King's Landing in search of opportunities and wealth. The region is also home to important institutions, such as the Red Keep, the Great Sept of Baelor, and the Dragonpit.

Due to its central location and political significance, the Crownlands have been the site of many political intrigues and power struggles throughout history.

Reach

The Reach is a fertile and prosperous region located in the southwestern part of Westeros. It is known for its bountiful harvests and lush farmlands, which have earned it the title of the "breadbasket of Westeros".

The Reach is ruled by House Tyrell, one of the most powerful noble houses in Westeros. The region is known for its strong agricultural traditions and its production of fine wines and fruits.

The Reach is also home to Highgarden, the seat of House Tyrell. Highgarden is renowned for its beautiful gardens and is considered one of the most splendid castles in Westeros.

Stormlands

The Stormlands are a rugged and stormy region located in the southeastern part of Westeros. It is known for its unpredictable weather, with frequent storms and heavy rainfall.

The Stormlands are ruled by House Baratheon, a noble house that rose to prominence during Robert's Rebellion. The region is known for its fierce warriors and its strong martial traditions.

The Stormlands are home to Storm's End, the ancestral seat of House Baratheon. Storm's End is a formidable castle, known for its massive curtain walls and its ability to withstand even the fiercest storms.

Dorne

Dorne is a desert region located in the southernmost part of Westeros. It is known for its arid climate, sandy dunes, and exotic culture.

Dorne is ruled by House Martell, one of the oldest noble houses in Westeros. The region has a unique social structure, with a strong emphasis on gender equality and individual freedoms.

The people of Dorne, known as the Dornish, are known for their resilience and their mastery of combat techniques such as the spear. Dorne is also famous for its spicy cuisine and its fine wines.

Free Cities

The Free Cities are a collection of independent city-states located in Essos. They are known for their diverse cultures, bustling markets, and influential trade networks.

Each Free City has its own unique characteristics and ruling families. Some of the prominent Free Cities include Pentos, Braavos, Volantis, and Myr.

These city-states are often involved in political intrigues and power struggles, making them important players in the politics of Essos.

Slaver's Bay

Slaver's Bay is a region located in Essos, known for its history of slavery and the slave trade. It is home to cities such as Astapor, Yunkai, and Meereen.

Daenerys Targaryen, also known as the Mother of Dragons, liberated Slaver's Bay and abolished slavery in these cities. However, the region continues to face challenges in transitioning to a free society.

Slaver's Bay is a complex and diverse region, with a mix of cultures and traditions. It serves as an important backdrop for the political and social dynamics of Essos.

Dothraki Sea

The Dothraki Sea is a vast grassland located in the eastern part of Essos. It is the ancestral homeland of the nomadic horse-riding Dothraki people.

The Dothraki are known for their fierce warrior culture and their mastery of horsemanship. They live in tribes called khalasars, led by powerful khals.

The Dothraki Sea is a challenging environment, with harsh weather conditions and limited resources. It is also a significant trade route, connecting the Free Cities to the eastern lands of Essos.

Braavos

Braavos is a powerful city-state located in Essos. It is known for its rich history, strong economy, and influential banking system.

Braavos is home to the Iron Bank, one of the most powerful financial institutions in the known world. The city is also known for its unique culture, including the Faceless Men, a secretive guild of assassins.

The city of Braavos is built on a group of islands connected by canals and bridges. It is a melting pot of different cultures and attracts people from all over Essos.

Qarth

Qarth is a wealthy city located in Essos, known as the "Greatest City that Ever Was or Will Be". It is a major trading hub and a center of culture and knowledge.

Qarth is famous for its opulent architecture, grandiose festivals, and the mysterious warlocks of the House of the Undying. The city is ruled by a council of merchant princes.

Qarth is a city of secrets and intrigue, with many factions vying for power and wealth. It is a crucial location in the journey of Daenerys Targaryen and her dragons.

Asshai

Asshai is a mysterious and ancient city located in the far east of Essos. It is known for its dark and mystical reputation.

Asshai is shrouded in secrecy and is said to be a center of dark magic and sorcery. The city is home to shadowbinders and other practitioners of arcane arts.

Very little is known about Asshai, as few people venture there and even fewer return. It is a place of legends and whispers, with a history that stretches back thousands of years.
